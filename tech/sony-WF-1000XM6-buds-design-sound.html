The new Sony WF-1000XM6 Earbuds have a less practical Design but better ANC, Sound and Mics


God-Tier ANC and Mic but just good Transparency and sound
Saturday, February 14, 2026 - by Soumya Roy


Earbuds from Sony are well-known for their top-tier Active Noise Cancellation, but never had the top-tier transparency and sound quality. The AirPods Pro buds are known for their remarkable natural transparency and great balanced sound signature. You should check out my AirPods Pro 3 vs Pro 2 comparison for more details on the AirPods. I liked the previous Sony WF-1000XM5 buds but never loved the design. They always looked kinda bulkier than other better players in the design aspect. The new Sony WF-1000XM6 buds have a promising smooth design, but the case looks less compact. So, let's take a look at the whole picture in this article, I am feeling kinda mixed about these new Sony WF-1000XM6 Earbuds.



Overall Thoughts

The big case doesn't offer big battery, which is unexpected. You get total 24 hours combining the buds and the case. The buds offer around 8 hours and the case gives you additional 2 full charges. The buds' 8-hours with ANC at a moderate volume is good but the new big case should have had one more full charge to justify the size. Sound quality is good but not great, and I just don't like the case design but I like the buds design, so I don't have any major negatives about these new Sony WF-1000XM6 buds. You can buy these new buds if you didn't have the previous one, but I won't upgrade from the XM5.



Totally a New Design

I was expecting a design change for the buds, but not for the case. The Sony WF-1000XM5 had a great case-design, it was a pretty compact case. The case had gotten pretty small and pocketable from the WF-1000XM4 to WF-1000XM5, but this new WF-1000XM6's case looks bulkier which is a step backward in my opinion. I was expecting an even more refined and smooth case, which will look more compact than the previous WF-1000XM5. The new case has pointy flat edges and looks quite a bit bigger, this is a backward direction in case-design and practicality.

So, I think this is totally a wrong approach in the case-design, but the buds' design is pretty cool. The new Sony WF-1000XM6 Earbuds look very different but better. Even though the new design may not look smaller but it is a more refined, smooth and a more compact design. The pill-shape kinda make them look small, cute and cooler than the previous WF-1000XM5 model. It's not a design that I love but I think it's a better design and a move forward in the earbuds' design. This new design is more comfortable and suitable for a wide range of ears.




Good Sound but not Impressive

Sony earbuds are not known for great sound quality, but they always offer good sound quality. These Sony WF-1000XM6 Earbuds are Sonyâ€™s premium-grade in-ear buds. So, one can expect a premium sound from them. But unfortunately, the sound is just decent like their previous model. Neither the old XM5 nor this new XM6 buds have great details and soundstage. However, they have decent bass and clarity, which is good enough for most people. There is no major issue with the sound quality of these new Sony WF-1000XM6 buds. Let's take a look at their frequency response analysis for a better understanding.



Sony has developed a new version of their 8.4 mm driver that is in the XM5 buds. This new driver for the WF-1000XM6 is soft, lightweight and delivers better audio performance. If you take a look at their bass response, it is on the bass-heavy side. It is an okay response but not a great response. There is a roll-off that starts well-before 20 Hz, which is not an extended bass response. You should expect an extended bass response but not a boosted response from a high-end earbud. The Sony WF-1000XM6 buds have good bass performance that will satisfy most people. You may like this little bassy and powerful sound.

This is not a major issue, but I think the lower midrange is a bit recessed. The response shows a dip but it's not a wide dip, so you will get less details from the lower mids. This is those sort of things that don't let buds have a great soundstage. The midrange is just fine and the overall clarity is pretty good. It has a little boosted bass and treble, but slightly recessed mids. So you have kind of a v-shaped sound signature here with the WF-1000XM6, and that gives a mediocre soundstage. The sound isn't overly bass or treble heavy, the mids are also not that bad. It's just not a very tastefully defined sound.



I would be pretty happy to have that kinda sound in a $100 buds, but these are not that cheap. They cost more than twice of that, which is kinda justified if we consider the whole picture. I think these XM6 Sony Earbuds have decent details in the bass and treble but lacks in the mids, but no major problem in the sound. Also, you can increase the midrange using their built-in custom eq. The default sound is good enough for people who loves party-type listening experience. But not gonna be good enough if you take your time while listening to music like an enthusiast.




Top-class ANC & Mic Quality

These XM6 buds have 8 mics meaning 4 mics on each earbud vs total 6 mics on the old XM5 buds. The previous model already had great ANC and this new XM6 takes it to even further. Especially there is great improvement in the microphone quality. The mics are very much involved in the call quality, ANC and transparency. Call quality is significantly better with improved noise reduction coming from your surroundings. Your friends will be able to hear you better when you are in traffic. The mics with Sony's algorithm help pick up your voice better and kinda like ignore the outside noise.

I would have called these new XM6 buds an upgrade if the case was just slightly more refined on top of the XM5's case as the old case was smaller than this new one. The new buds have very smooth curved shape, which is totally opposite of the new case's design. The case and buds don't match up in terms of this new design.
